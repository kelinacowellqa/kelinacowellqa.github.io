What I Do

I provide focused QA testing for indie teams and solo developers who need practical, structured feedback without a bloated corporate testing process getting in the way.

My work focuses on the things players actually feel: confusing onboarding, unclear systems, broken progression, unstable core loops, input issues, accessibility barriers and bugs that need clear evidence before a developer can fix them.

Each pass is timeboxed, evidence-driven and adapted to the build. I can work with Jira, Trello, Notion, Google Sheets, Discord reports or whatever lightweight system your team already uses.

Freelance QA Services

Demo & Early Build QA

Focused testing for demos, alphas, betas and early builds, aimed at finding blockers, confusing flows and high-impact player-facing issues before wider release.

Exploratory Testing

Charter-based sessions designed around your game systems, risks and current development priorities, rather than generic test templates pasted onto the wrong project.

Bug Reporting & Evidence

Clear, reproducible bug reports with steps, expected result, actual result, severity, repro rate, environment details and short video evidence where useful.

Player Experience & Clarity Review

Testing focused on where player understanding breaks, including onboarding, UI feedback, system communication, cause and effect, and decision-making under pressure.

Accessibility-Focused QA

Practical accessibility checks informed by APX training, including readability, input strain, cognitive load, feedback redundancy, subtitles and recovery behaviour.

Regression & Fix Verification

Retesting fixes, checking patch-note claims against the live build, and validating that changes have not broken nearby systems or core gameplay flows.

Sample QA Work

These case studies show the kind of QA output I can provide: structured scope, practical testing, clear findings and evidence-backed reporting.

Corpus Et Spiritus - Targeted QA Pass: Card Systems & State Integrity (PC)

What I tested: A short, targeted QA pass on a PC build, focused on card resolution, input handling (click vs drag), and card lifecycle clarity under rapid player interaction.

Key finding: Card resolution remained stable, but rapid clicking introduced input instability, causing failed triggers and unintended multi-card activation during subsequent drag interactions.

Why it matters: Systems can be technically stable but still break under player pressure. Input inconsistency and unclear card states reduce player trust and make fast decision-making unreliable.

View QA Case Study

Cult of PiN - Live Demo Exploratory QA

What I tested: A short exploratory QA pass on a PC demo, focused on ritual systems, player clarity, timing behaviour, system stacking and progression flow.

Key finding: Key systems were technically stable but not always clearly communicated, especially when rituals, multipliers and boss effects overlapped during high-intensity gameplay.

Why it matters: Not every QA finding is a crash or blocker. Sometimes the problem is that the player cannot understand what the game is doing quickly enough to make good decisions.

Freelance Game QA FAQ

Do you only work with indie developers?

No. Indie teams and solo developers are my main focus, but the same structured QA approach can also support small studios, student teams, jam teams and early-stage projects.

What platforms can you test on?

I can test PC, Android mobile and standalone VR on Meta Quest 3. Platform availability depends on the build, access method and project requirements.

Can you use Jira?

Yes. I can use Jira for bug reporting where applicable, but I can also adapt to lightweight indie workflows such as Trello, Notion, Google Sheets, Discord-based reporting or your existing tracker.

What do you deliver after a QA pass?

Deliverables depend on scope, but can include bug reports, reproduction steps, severity notes, repro rate, environment details, video clips, screenshots, session notes, summary findings and practical recommendations.

Do you provide playtesting or QA?

My focus is QA, not casual playtesting. I can include player experience analysis, but the output is structured around reproducible issues, risks, clarity problems and actionable findings for development.
